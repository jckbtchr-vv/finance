02

Lease Mechanics

A lease payment has two parts: (1) the value the car loses while you drive it, and (2) the interest fee for borrowing an expensive asset. You're paying for both.

03

Framework

Value Lost (Depreciation)

  • Sticker price minus what it's worth when you return it.
  • The biggest chunk of your lease payment.
  • Cars that hold value better = cheaper leases.

Interest (Money Factor)

  • The fee for using someone else's $50k car.
  • Money factor × 2400 = the APR you'd see on a loan.
  • 0.0025 = about 6% interest rate.

Value Left (Residual)

  • What the dealer thinks the car will be worth at lease end.
  • Higher residual = lower monthly payment.
  • This is why Toyotas lease cheaper than luxury brands.
