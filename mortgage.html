02

Interest Mechanics

Interest is front-loaded. In the early years of a mortgage, the majority of your payment goes toward interest, not principal. This visualization shows how that ratio shifts over the term of the loan.

03

Long-term Impact

Small changes in interest rates result in massive differences in total cost. A 1% increase in rate on a $500,000 30-year mortgage adds approximately $100,000 to the total interest paid.

04

Framework

The Principal

  • The raw cost of the asset.
  • The number that decreases as you pay down the debt.
  • Equity is the portion of principal you own.

The Interest

  • The cost of borrowing time.
  • Front-loaded in the amortization schedule.
  • Highly sensitive to the annual percentage rate.

The Term

  • The duration of the agreement.
  • Longer terms lower monthly payments but increase total cost.
  • Time is the multiplier for interest.
